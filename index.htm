Quantum Schrödinger (QS) is not just another token — it's an experiment in value, perception, and choice. Inspired by Schrödinger's famous thought experiment, QS lives in a state of uncertainty — both alive and dead — until you decide to act. Its core idea is simple: the token's state depends on the observer. Just like the cat in the box, its future is undefined until you look inside. In QS, this philosophical concept becomes real through three mechanics: Staking — Hold and earn as the project evolves. Lock — Temporarily remove tokens from circulation, increasing scarcity. Burn — Choose to destroy tokens permanently, reducing supply. These actions aren't just features — they are observations. Every choice you make changes the outcome, shifts the supply, and moves the narrative. QS is an interactive metaphor, a financial and conceptual experiment wrapped in a meme. Welcome to a token that exists because you look at it.
Schrödinger's cat is a famous thought experiment in quantum physics that illustrates the idea of superposition: a cat placed in a sealed box with a radioactive atom, poison, and a detector is considered both alive and dead until someone opens the box and observes it, collapsing the possibilities into one outcome.
